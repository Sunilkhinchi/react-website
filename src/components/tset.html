ANNEXURE D

SUPPORT TERMS

Company shall provide support services in accordance with the terms of this Annexure, which may be amended from time to time by Company. 1. Support Model Company will offer a standard support model with 24x5 online and email support Monday to Friday by leveraging follow the sun model. This should provide coverage for Customer’s global sites between Monday 12am Pacific to Friday 6pm Pacific time zone. Phone support is available for escalations and cases where the ticket remains unresolved after the target resolution time has passed. All support will be provided in English. This document describes the support model, SLAs and specific details of how Customer will be provided support for the global deployment of Services described in Annexure A. L1 Support – Provided by Customer
  • L1 Support will be provided by Customer’s local IT admin for facilities team for the basic user queries, training, access and monitoring for Exit door monitoring system.
  • Training session will be recorded and videos and documentation will be provided for the local IT admin users for future use.
  • The training videos and centralized user guide will help the basic IP Camera Setup, Access and performance issues.
  • Customer will use its internal ticket management system for the L1 support
  • Camera installation and hardware issues will be handled by the Customer
  • Camera malfunctions will be handled by the local IT team of the Customer; Company alerts for unreachable or inactive cameras will be shared with Customer’s assigned contacts
  L2 Support – Provided by Company
  • L2 Support will be provided by Company’s team, the issue will be reported in the Company helpdesk(https://oloid.freshdesk.com/support/home) or by emailing support@oloid.ai
  • The incident management tickets will be raised by the local IT Admin, after initial assessment for basic issues. Setup, Access, and performance issue which are not resolved by common documentation. Any site-specific calibration and hardware upgrades.
  • Company will work with location IT and Facilities team for needed support for fixing the issue.
  • If any code change, config changes needed Company’s team will publish the timeline and if applicable any alternate solution approach.
  L3 Support – Provided by Company
  • L3 Support Tickets will be raised by business owners, centralized IT team, security and facilities team.
  • The issue is major process, config changes which has impact on multiple sites. System failure, performance issues at enterprise level.
  • Incident management tickets created and assigned priority.
  • Company’s team will setup a review session with Customer team for understanding issue.
  • Company will do RCA, Change Management Log, update the incident ticket for resolution timeline.
  • Customer’s configuration document will be updated and any process changes will be updated in centralized location for further reference.
  2. Creating/Logging Support Tickets All users and managers of the Company’s Software can directly access Company’s online knowledge base at https://oloid.freshdesk.com/support/home, including the latest installation guides, training materials and FAQs. In addition, users can avail support from Company’s technical support team by the following means:
  • Ticket Center: To report a bug or unexpected behavior of the platform, end users (learners, managers) can raise a support ticket at the help site: https://oloid.freshdesk.com/support/home .
  • Email: Alternatively, users can directly get in touch with the Licensor Technical Support team, by sending an email to support@oloid.ai
Once submitted a ticket number will be automatically assigned in an automated response email. Subsequently, a technical specialist from the Company will directly get in touch with the user on the email address submitted in the ticket or through available contact options, within the applicable initial response time described in the SLA (service level agreement). Tickets submitted with non-business email will not ignored and not routed to Company’s support.   3. Accessing Support Resources and Self-Help Unlimited 24-hour Access to Company’s Support Web Site (https://oloid.freshdesk.com/support/home) with the following features:
  • FAQs, as available
  • Online Knowledge Base, as available
  • On-line incident logging/ticket creation
  • Articles and White papers
  4. Severity Levels and Response Times All support tickets and emails sent to Company’s technical support team are categorized into priority levels, according to the guidelines listed below. Please note that the descriptions below are guidelines and may not cover every possible condition or technical situation. Company’s technical support team reserves the right to assign or re-assign the severity level for the support tickets and emails based on reasonable judgement and guidelines below. In case the Customer would like to upgrade the severity level of a particular ticket, this can be done through a mutual discussion with the stakeholders of the Customer and Company.  
Severity Level Definition
P1 Critical production issue preventing all or most users from using Platform or a business-critical functionality, with no workaround available
P2 Major functionality is impacted or significant performance degradation is experienced by a large number of users, and no reasonable workaround is available
P3 System performance issue, task requests or bug affecting some users, with short-term workaround available
P4 Issues and requests without significant, immediate-term impact on system performance and / or acceptable workaround available

Using Go-to-meeting, zoom or some other equivalent online mechanism (provided it is at no charge to the customer), Company’s customer support representatives may initiate secure, interactive, online sessions to diagnose, troubleshoot and solve support issues. Failure to provide such information and/or access may result in extended resolution times.   5.   Definitions
  • First response time: The timeframe within which the Company’s technical support team will respond to a support ticket or email sent by a user. The first response will typically provide an initial suggestion towards fixing the reported issue or request for additional information to help diagnose the reported issue better.
  • Target resolution time: The timeframe within which the Company’s technical support team estimates to provide a resolution to the reported issue. Resolution is defined as an answer, solution, completed change request, or a workaround that resolves the reported issue.
 
  • While Company will make all commercially reasonable efforts to resolve an issue within the target resolution time, there could be exceptional circumstances (usually attributable to factors outside Company’s direct control) resulting in the resolution time extending beyond the target resolution time mentioned above. In such situations, the technical support team will inform the user about the delay and work with them to control the impact as much as reasonably possible.
  • In a situation where a ticket is not resolved within the target resolution time, the last section of this document provides the procedure for escalation and resolution
  • Ticket submission: Ticket submission is the event when an issue is reported by a user to Company’s technical support team by means of one of the available options described above
  • Closing of Ticket: An open ticket will be marked as closed after the resolution/fix has been confirmed by Customer, over email or phone or on-site in person
  • Business Hours: Company’s standard business hours for Support Services is Monday 12:00AM to Friday 6:00 PM Pacific Time Zone.

  • "Personal Information Request" means a request from a consumer under the CCPA to exercise the right to know or right to delete.
  • “Security Breach” means an unauthorized acquisition or use of unsecured Personal Information that creates a substantial risk of identity theft or fraud against an individual. For clarity, a good faith acquisition of Personal Information by an employee or agent of Company is not a Security Breach unless such employee or agent uses or discloses the Personal Information in an unauthorized manner.
